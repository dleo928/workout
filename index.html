Programme guide

Principles behind the plan.

Warm-up

Every session starts with a targeted 5-minute warm-up specific to that day's muscles and movements. Don't skip it — it primes joints and reduces injury risk, especially for shoulders and knees.

Priority areas

Back, shoulders, and legs get the most volume and heaviest compound movements. Both modes lead each session with priority muscles before fatigue sets in.

Progressive overload

The only driver of long-term muscle growth. Every 1–2 weeks, add one rep or a small weight increase on your main lifts. A mental note of last session is enough.

Zone 2 cardio

Your VO₂ max of 52 is excellent. Zone 2 (~130–145 bpm) maintains mitochondrial density and metabolic health. Conversational pace — if you can't speak in sentences, ease off.

Weekly tracking

Each week (Mon–Sun) is its own record. Incomplete weeks stay in history in amber. A new week starts automatically on Monday. Tap "Partial" if you only finish part of a session.

Rest and recovery

Muscle grows during recovery. Aim for 7–9 hrs sleep. Protein ~0.8–1g per kg bodyweight. Space strength days with at least one rest day between them.

Joint health

Floor press instead of bench, goblet squats, slow eccentrics on lateral raises, dead hangs for spine and shoulder health. Longevity is built in, not added on.

Equipment tip

A doorframe pull-up bar (~€20) is the single best upgrade — used for dead hangs and eventually pull-ups.
